What We Offer

Our Income Tax & TDS Compliance Services

ITR Filing (All Forms)

Accurate and timely filing of Income Tax Returns for individuals (ITR-1 to ITR-4), businesses (ITR-5, 6, 7), and LLPs across all categories.

TDS Return Filing

Quarterly filing of TDS returns (24Q, 26Q, 27Q, 27EQ) and issuance of Form 16/16A to employees and deductees.

Advance Tax Planning

Calculation and timely payment of advance tax instalments to avoid interest under Sections 234B and 234C.

Tax Optimisation

Strategic planning using available exemptions, deductions (80C, 80D, HRA, etc.) to legally minimise your tax liability.

Scrutiny & Notice Handling

Professional representation before Income Tax Officers and handling of scrutiny assessments, demands, and rectification requests.

Form 15CA/15CB

Assistance with remittances to non-residents including Form 15CA/15CB filing for foreign remittances as per RBI guidelines.

What You Need

Documents for ITR Filing

  • PAN Card & Aadhaar
  • Form 16 / Form 16A (from employer/deductors)
  • Bank account statements & passbook
  • Home loan statement (for housing interest deduction)
  • Investment proofs (80C, 80D, 80G, etc.)
  • Capital gains statements (shares, property, mutual funds)
  • Business/profession income details & P&L statement
  • Previous year's ITR (for reference and carry-forward losses)

Note: Our team will provide a precise document checklist tailored to your specific situation after an initial consultation.

Key Advantages

Why Timely Tax Filing Matters

  • Avoid Penalties — Late filing attracts penalties up to ₹10,000 under Section 234F, plus interest on outstanding tax.
  • Carry Forward Losses — Filing on time enables you to carry forward business and capital losses to offset future income.
  • Loan & Visa Applications — ITR is a key document required for home loans, business loans, and international visa applications.
  • Refund Claims — Timely filing ensures you receive your TDS refunds quickly with applicable interest.
  • Clean Tax Record — A compliant tax history builds credibility with banks, investors, and government authorities.
  • Avoid Prosecution — Persistent non-filing can lead to prosecution under the Income Tax Act.
